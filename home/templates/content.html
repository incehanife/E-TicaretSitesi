Sport strengthens the heart and blood circulation system.

During sports, the need for blood and oxygen in the body increases. For those who do not do sports for the first time and run for the first time, the body has difficulty in breathing, and sufficient blood cannot be pumped to the body. It's normal. But by the way, the body has already begun to prepare for the next run and immediately after the first exercise, it starts to store glycogen in the muscles. In the following days and months, the heart starts to breathe more easily and pump blood to the body more easily during sports.

Sport burns fat. Goodbye to the kilos!

Running for 10 minutes burns more fat than doing 10 minutes of exercise in the fitness room. In running over 30 minutes, the body obtains the necessary energy by using the fat stored in the body. Running for 60 to 90 minutes 3-4 times a week causes the body to burn about 2000 calories. If running is done on an empty stomach, the body starts to burn fat immediately. In addition, drinking water and unsweetened tea after sports accelerates the burning of fat even more.

Sport makes people happy.

Why do we feel better after sports? During sports (eg jogging), there is a significant increase in the secretion of two hormones (endorphin and serotonin) called "happiness hormone". This increase, which helps to relieve the physical fatigue that appears in the muscles with running, also has significant benefits that support psychology ... In addition to the support of sports to cope with difficulties and the necessary self-confidence, all these positive physical and psychological effects are perceived as rewards in the brain and thus the person doing sports is happy. is happening. Studies have proven that people who do sports are happier.

According to statistics, sports relieves back and waist pain.

Our skeletal muscles are needed for us for life and we have to take good care of them. The sedentary lifestyle is the worst enemy of the muscles. Therefore, back pain is inevitable for those who prefer a sedentary life (laziness) with office workers who have to sit at a desk due to their work. The best way to strengthen the waist and back muscles is to exercise. The most ideal sport in this regard is swimming or at least walking in a water-filled pool. These types of activities enable the smallest and deepest muscles to work by stimulating them and thus ensure continuity in their structures. In addition, exercises to strengthen the waist and muscles can be applied at home. For example, exercises made by trying to keep balance on a light-hard sponge called Airex-Kissen are effective in strengthening the waist and back muscles. Apart from that, these exercises can also be done on a very soft ball or a folded towel.
